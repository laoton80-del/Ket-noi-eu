# VIONA Human Constellation Design System

**Document type:** App-wide visual doctrine  
**Audience:** Product, design, engineering, and AI agents implementing VIONA surfaces  
**Status:** HC.DOC.1 — design doctrine (documentation only)  
**Relationship:** Complements `VIONA_OPERATING_PROTOCOL.md`, `VIONA_GLOBAL_EXPERIENCE_MANIFESTO.md`, `VIONA_FASHION_TECH_VISUAL_SYSTEM.md`, and **`VIONA_NEON_GLASS_CARD_SYSTEM.md`** (app-wide card tier, accent, hover, and glass-surface rules). When visual polish conflicts with signed production truth, **operating protocol and blueprint win**.

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines VIONA’s **Human Constellation** visual direction for the **entire app**, not a single screen or universe.

VIONA is a **global Vietnamese companion OS**: a human service constellation that connects life, work, learning, travel, safety, and commerce across borders. The interface should feel like a **premium AI and business automation layer** wrapped in a **trusted cross-border life assistant**—warm, cinematic, and calm—not a cold control panel or a vertical demo.

Human Constellation means:

- **People and services** are the story; technology is the connective tissue.
- **Routes, arcs, and nodes** suggest relationship and reach without rigid dashboards or fake “network operations” theater.
- **Maturity is honest**; beauty must not imply production behavior that does not exist.

This doctrine governs **visual language, component behavior, copy posture, and rollout discipline**. It does **not** authorize payment, auth, routing, AI runtime, or database changes by itself.

---

## 2. What VIONA should not look like

Reject patterns that mis-position the product or erode trust:

| Anti-pattern | Why it fails |
|--------------|--------------|
| **Admin dashboard** | VIONA is a companion OS, not an internal ops console for end users. |
| **Crypto wallet** | VIO credits and wallet surfaces are in-app utility, not speculative finance. |
| **Booking-only app** | Commerce and booking are mini-apps inside a broader life graph. |
| **Travel-only app** | Travel is one constellation arm, not the whole product story. |
| **AI demo app** | AI is embedded assistance with honest pilot/demo labeling. |
| **Cyberpunk / gaming UI** | Thick neon, rainbow edges, and arcade glow read as entertainment, not trusted companion software. |
| **Generic SaaS dark mode** | Flat gray cards, stock dashboard grids, and anonymous “enterprise” chrome lack human warmth and Vietnamese-global identity. |

If a surface could belong to any anonymous B2B product, it is **not** Human Constellation.

## 3. Core visual language

### 3.1 Base atmosphere

- **Dark cinematic premium base** — deep navy / ink canvas with controlled elevation, not pure black void or muddy gray wash.
- **Human-global warmth** — gold and soft bloom carry companion feeling; cold blue alone reads as infrastructure, not care.
- **Soft bloom and depth** — offset shadows, satin highlights, and thin glass veils; avoid large radial glow blobs.

### 3.2 Constellation motif

- **Warm gold constellation arcs** — curved luminous trails that suggest routes between people, places, and services.
- **Cyan / blue service intelligence** — focus, language, travel context, and AI assistance accents.
- **Emerald trust nodes** — identity, verification, profile completion, and safety-ready states.
- **Magenta / red only for SOS, demo, or urgent** — never as default decoration.

### 3.3 Hard bans

- No **rigid network grid** as primary decoration.
- No **large abstract orbs** or circular washes behind content.
- No **rainbow** or multi-color edge treatments.
- No **thick neon borders** or cyberpunk frame overload.
- No **one card, many accents** — each component has **one primary accent** unless a documented exception (e.g. SOS).

Motifs **support content**; they do not compete with forms, CTAs, or safety messaging.

## 4. Color semantics

Use color as **meaning**, not decoration.

### Gold

- Brand and premium hierarchy  
- Primary CTA emphasis  
- VIO display and commerce anchor (without implying cash or withdrawable money)  
- Champagne lines, constellation arcs, and command-rail framing  

### Cyan / Blue

- AI assistance and intelligence surfaces  
- Global and cross-border context  
- Travel and movement  
- Language and Smart Trio clarity  
- Focus states and secondary glass actions  

### Emerald

- Identity and profile trust  
- Verified or completion-positive states  
- Local life and community-safe emphasis  
- Safety-ready **guidance** (not live emergency resolution claims)  

### Magenta / Red

- **SOS** and lifeline seriousness  
- Urgent attention within policy  
- Demo / special surfaces only when explicitly labeled  
- Never the default accent for routine commerce or navigation  

### Violet

- **Academy** and learning  
- Creative AI and practice modes  
- Soft, limited use — not a second brand color

## 5. Screen-level application

Apply the same constellation grammar with **universe-appropriate density**. Hero and licensed photography rules from existing packs still apply where documented.

### Home / Hub

- Command rail: brand, greeting/time, language, VIO credits, SOS, account in **one chip system**.  
- Living hero: **preserve licensed hero and dynamic hero imagery**; improve frame, left readability veil, gold accent, subtle cyan edge, CTA hierarchy, and honest status chips.  
- Mini-app cards: Local, Travel, Academy, Business as **connected service nodes** with one accent each.  
- Quick actions: compact “VIONA for you” strip; no fake staffing or 24/7 claims.

### Login / Welcome

- Gateway into the global ecosystem: constellation field behind brand, not a sparse admin form.  
- SOS magenta remains **limited and serious**.  
- Demo entry visible but not dominant.

### SetupProfile / Identity

- Structured identity personalization: faint mapping field, trust spine, emerald/cyan nodes — not a plain admin form.  
- Form rows stay clean; decoration stays behind content.

### Account

- Service graph / account intelligence control center: thin connection language behind cards, gold anchor near profile/VIO areas.  
- No large circular blobs; wallet and checkout behavior unchanged by visual packs.

### SOS

- **Serious lifeline** visual lane: magenta/red discipline, no gamification, no fake dispatch or “active protection” theater.  
- Copy and interaction truth follow SOS product specs and operating protocol.

### Local

- Emerald-led constellation: community, services, daily life.  
- **Lite** or **Pilot** labels when surfaces are preview or gated.

### Travel

- Cyan-led constellation: direction, movement, return, safety-aware guidance.  
- **Lite** until production readiness is signed.

### Academy

- Violet-soft constellation: family learning and AI practice.  
- **Demo** where automation or curriculum is not production-backed.

### Merchant / B2B / AI Receptionist

- Gold commerce anchor with cyan operational intelligence.  
- Pilot and gated labels on automation, payments, and lead flows.  
- No fake booking, payment, or inventory outcomes.

### B2B Wholesale / E-shop Import

- Structured catalog and trade graph — not a fake marketplace.  
- Supplier, SKU, margin, and compliance truth in copy and status chips.

### Admin / Ops

- May use denser tables and diagnostics, but **must not leak** admin-dashboard aesthetics into consumer Home or auth shells.  
- Internal tools follow the same honesty rules for maturity labels.

## 6. Component doctrine

### Buttons

- **Primary:** gold, premium, readable on dark; one primary per view region.  
- **Secondary:** glass / cyan subtle border; no neon frame.  
- **Destructive / SOS:** red/magenta only where policy allows.  
- Disabled states stay legible; do not fake enabled production paths.

### Cards

Follow **`VIONA_NEON_GLASS_CARD_SYSTEM.md`** for app-wide Neon Glass behavior: three intensity tiers (Hero / Secondary / Utility), semantic accent families, single-surface glass anatomy, hover reveal, and banned nested slabs.

At Human Constellation level:

- Dark glass or elevated ink surface; **thin luminous border** (crisp inset stroke—not thick gaming neon).  
- **One accent per card** (family wash, icon halo, chip border, or edge—not competing rims).  
- Image cards: licensed art only; scrim for text readability; no stock-ad collage look.  
- Wallet, pricing, checkout, and dense forms stay **Utility** tier unless a signed pack says otherwise.

### Chips

- Shared geometry across command rail, filters, and quick actions.  
- Thin gold or cyan border; soft fill; SOS chip visually distinct.

### Status badges

- Honest maturity: **Lite**, **Demo**, **Pilot**, **Gated**, **Coming soon**, **Frozen**, **Needs review**.  
- Tone mapping: cyan (lite/global), emerald (pilot/trust where appropriate), violet (demo/academy), gold (coming soon / muted), red (urgent only).  
- Avoid **Active** unless production-signed.

### Backgrounds

- Constellation fields: arcs, small nodes, curved trails — **no rigid grid**, **no large orbs**.  
- Fallback dark canvas always present for readability.  
- Overlay scrim above imagery when text sits on photography or generated fields.

### Icons

- Single-accent halo or thin ring; offset depth preferred over radial glow.  
- SOS iconography serious, not playful.

### Image cards

- Do not replace licensed heroes without an approved asset pack.  
- Overlays and frames may change; **pixel content of approved heroes does not** in HC.HOME.1-class packs.

### Command rails

- Integrated greeting, time, and utilities — not a detached dashboard header.  
- VIONA wordmark strong but not oversized.

### Forms

- Clean rows; identity mapping stays in the backdrop layer.  
- Focus markers: small cyan or emerald node — not full-row neon.

### Modals

- Dark glass sheet; thin border; one accent.  
- Safety and payment modals use copy discipline from Section 8; no visual pack changes settlement logic.

## 7. Production safety copy rules

Visual packs **must not** introduce or reinforce unsafe claims unless **production readiness is signed** and documented.

### Banned or restricted claims (unless backed)

- **Active** (as in “feature is live everywhere”)  
- **Live** (staffed support, emergency resolution, or AI always on)  
- **24/7 AI support**  
- **180+ countries active** (or similar scale claims)  
- **Safety Assist Active** (when automation or dispatch is not production-ready)  
- **Fake social proof** (avatar stacks, user counts, “thousands online”)  
- **Payment success** or confirmed paid state without server truth  
- **AI booked / paid / refunded** without governed rails  

### Safer labels (preferred)

- Global Lite preview  
- Safety guide ready  
- AI pilot surface  
- Smart Trio ready  
- Lite  
- Demo  
- Pilot  
- Gated  
- Coming soon  
- Frozen  
- Needs review  

Copy and i18n changes in visual packs stay **Class A** only when they do not change routing, payment, or auth semantics. Ambiguous commercial claims escalate per operating protocol.

## 11. VIONA Neon Glass Card System (GLOBAL.NEON.CARDS.1)

**Pack scope:** doctrine and reusable **named token presets** only. Do **not** wire tokens into screens, frames, or routes in this pack. **Home** remains the visual source of truth for energy and proportion; later packs adopt screen-by-screen.

### 11.1 System definition

**Neon Glass** is VIONA’s app-wide card language: dark cinematic glass, vivid semantic accent families, crisp luminous borders, soft outer glow, controlled icon and chip lighting, and hover that reveals background through translucency. The target is **premium hi-tech companion software**, not gaming chaos.

### 11.2 Intensity tiers

| Tier | Name | Role |
|------|------|------|
| **1** | Hero / Primary | Strongest glow, brightest edge, largest internal wash, strongest hover reveal — hero cards, main destination cards, flagship service entry. |
| **2** | Secondary | Moderate glow, clear accent border, visible icon/chip glow — feature cards, helper cards, service grids, most interactive nodes. |
| **3** | Utility / Dense | Restrained glow, readability first — forms, wallet, pricing, checkout, settings, legal, dense text. |

Do **not** make every card Tier 1.

### 11.3 Accent families

| Family | Semantics |
|--------|-----------|
| **Emerald** | Local, community, trust, human support |
| **Cyan** | Travel, discovery, movement, nearby, language / network |
| **Gold** | Premium, merchant, business, important CTA, VIP |
| **Violet** | AI, academy, pilot intelligence, learning |
| **Magenta / Red** | SOS, safety, urgent, emergency **only** |

One primary family per card. **No** magenta/red on normal service cards.

### 11.4 Card anatomy

Each card is **one continuous glass surface**:

- Translucent dark glass fill (tier sets default and hover alpha)  
- Crisp luminous border (thin inset stroke, not thick neon)  
- Subtle internal light wash (corner / top highlight)  
- Icon glow via accent ring or chip border  
- Status chip with accent-tinted fill and border  
- Arrow / CTA ink that brightens on hover  
- Hover lift (tier-scaled) and **background reveal** (lower fill alpha, backdrop participation on web)  

### 11.5 Anti-patterns

- No nested slabs or card-inside-card opaque panels  
- No red/magenta on routine commerce or service cards  
- No thick neon, rainbow rims, or full-card text glow  
- No opaque cards that hide the page field unless Tier 3 dense utility requires it  
- No collapsing the app into a single cyan tone  

### 11.6 Rollout rules

1. **Screen-by-screen packs** with narrow allow-lists; no app-wide rewrite in one change.  
2. **Home** stays the reference for vivid energy and tier proportion.  
3. **Local** first polish after Home-aligned tokens exist.  
4. Then **Travel**, **Academy**, **Business** in separate packs.  
5. **Wallet, pricing, checkout:** Tier 3 Utility only unless a signed pack explicitly elevates a single marketing hero.  
6. **No** backend, API, auth, payment, wallet math, booking, route, or navigation logic changes in visual packs.  
7. **No commit** without user approval.

**Token foundation (this pack):** `src/components/viona/neonCardTokens.ts` — named color, alpha, glow, border, hover, and tier presets only; no behavior and no screen imports until a later pack.

## 12. Neon Global Network Card System (VIONA.NEON.NETWORK.CARDS.1)

**Pack scope:** Local-first implementation of hi-tech **global network neon-glass** cards. Home remains the visual energy reference; other universes adopt in later packs.

### 12.1 System definition

Cards are **translucent deep-navy glass** with **crisp bright neon borders**, soft outer glow, internal accent wash, faint **network-node lighting** (corner radial + diagonal sweep via gradients only), glowing icon chips, luminous status chips, and arrow/CTA glow. Hover lifts the card, brightens the edge, expands glow, and **reveals more of the page background** through lower fill alpha.

### 12.2 Intensity tiers (Local mapping)

| Tier | Local use |
|------|-----------|
| **Hero** | Local intro / hero card |
| **Service** | Journey cards, nearby service grid, guide cards, classified listings |
| **Utility** | Dense forms, wallet, pricing, checkout (later packs) |

### 12.3 Accent families

Same semantics as Section 11.3. **Magenta/red** is SOS-only; VIP classified listings may use **gold**, not red.

### 12.4 Hover (web)

Lift 1–2px, brighter border, expanded glow, more luminous icon and CTA, background more visible through glass, **140–200ms** ease-out transition, no layout jump.

### 12.5 Anti-patterns

No nested slabs, card-inside-card, opaque field-blocking rectangles, weak gray-only borders, thick rainbow neon, or red on normal service cards.

### 12.6 Rollout

Local cards first (`LocalConstellationFrame`, Local commerce block, Local screen). Then Travel, Academy, Business in separate packs. **No** backend, payment, booking, wallet, or route changes in visual packs.

**Implementation anchors:** `src/components/viona/neonNetworkCardTokens.ts`, `src/components/local/localConstellationTokens.ts`, `src/components/local/LocalConstellationFrame.tsx`.

## 13. VIONA Global Network UI (VIONA.GLOBAL.NETWORK.UI.1)

**Pack scope:** app-wide **visual direction** and **named token foundation** only. Do **not** wire tokens into screens, routes, or product logic in this pack. The latest VIONA global-network style-board is the energy reference: premium dark navy cinematic base, luminous map/network arcs, vivid neon technology colors, glassmorphism, controlled glow, premium typography, large clean VIONA logo, high-tech global intelligence mood.

### 13.1 Visual identity name

**VIONA Global Network UI** is the app-wide visual layer for premium high-tech surfaces: a distinctive global companion product, not a safe dark dashboard.

### 13.2 Core visual principles

- Dark cinematic navy base with visible global light-net / connection arcs  
- Translucent glass surfaces over the field (not opaque slabs)  
- Vivid neon accents with clear glow hierarchy  
- High-contrast typography (bright titles, readable body)  
- Large clean logo presence on major entry surfaces  
- Premium hi-tech global intelligence — **not** gaming chaos  

### 13.3 Five accent families (role mapping)

| Family | Roles |
|--------|--------|
| **Violet / Purple** | AI, academy, intelligence, prediction, learning |
| **Electric Blue / Cyan** | Travel, movement, network, language, discovery, connection |
| **Emerald / Green** | Local, community, growth, support, trust |
| **Gold / Yellow** | Premium, merchant, business, protect, credits, primary CTA |
| **Magenta / Red** | SOS, urgent, safety, emergency, heart/care lane **only** |

**Rule:** Magenta/red is **not** for normal service cards.

### 13.4 Card anatomy

Premium VIONA cards support: translucent deep glass fill; crisp neon border; inner top highlight; soft outer glow; internal global-light wash; subtle network line/node impression; glowing icon chip; luminous status chip; arrow/CTA glow; hover lift; hover background reveal. One continuous surface — no nested slabs.

### 13.5 Card intensity tiers

| Tier | Role |
|------|------|
| **1 — Hero / Primary** | Largest impact, strongest scene and glow — hero cards, main route cards, destination cards |
| **2 — Service / Feature** | Vivid color identity, moderate scene glow, clear hover — Local / Travel / Academy / Business cards |
| **3 — Utility / Dense** | Restrained glow, readability first — forms, wallet, checkout, pricing, settings, legal |

### 13.6 Typography direction

- Large confident headings; bright white / ivory titles  
- Muted blue-gray body copy; accent labels in uppercase tracking  
- Gold / cyan / violet / emerald accents by semantic role  
- Avoid gray-on-navy low contrast; no excessive text glow  
- Premium tech editorial UI, not generic dashboard type  

### 13.7 Logo direction

- **Global-tech wordmark:** large clean **VIONA** lockup with wide tracking; ivory-white or soft gold-white on dark global-network fields.  
- **Brand anchor, not a badge:** confident scale in command rails and auth entry; no heavy dark or purple logo boxes.  
- **One logo per viewport** — no duplicated lockups in the same screen.  
- **Transparent or softly blended** logo surface; optional subtle gold glow; optional subtitle or accent underline for section identity (e.g. Local caption), not a second logo.  
- Prefer reusable `VionaBrandLockup` variants (`hero`, `header`, `compact`, `iconOnly` when asset-only is needed).  
- Avoid stretched PNG plates, repeated logo assets, and boxed treatments that fight the background.

### 13.8 Hover / interaction (web)

Card lifts slightly; edge brightens; glow expands; fill more transparent so the **network field** reads through; icon, chip, and arrow brighten; **140–200ms** ease-out; no layout jump.

### 13.9 Anti-patterns

Safe flat navy boxes; thin gray borders only; opaque cards hiding the background; nested slabs; duplicated logos; all cards same cyan; red on non-SOS cards; thick rainbow neon; unreadable glowing text; generic dashboard look.

### 13.10 Rollout order

1. **VIONA.GLOBAL.LOCAL.1** — Local first  
2. Travel  
3. Academy  
4. Business / Merchant  
5. Account / Auth / Identity refinement after  
6. Wallet / pricing / checkout — **Tier 3 only**  
7. SOS — magenta/red safety lane only  

**Token foundation (this pack):** `src/components/viona/globalNetworkTokens.ts` — named canvas, typography, accent, glass, border, glow, hover, tier, and role-mapping presets. Card-tier detail may align with `neonNetworkCardTokens.ts` in later packs; **no screen imports** until an explicit rollout pack.

## 14. VIONA Global Light Network UI (VIONA.GLOBAL.LIGHT.NETWORK.1)

**Pack scope:** official **design foundation** and **named token exports** for the VIONA Global Light Network visual direction. Doctrine only in this pack — **no screen rollout**, **no route wiring**, **no backend, auth, payment, wallet, pricing, checkout, or booking** changes.

### 14.1 Visual identity name

**VIONA Global Light Network UI** is the app-wide premium hi-tech layer: a **dark cinematic navy** field with **sharp global light-network backgrounds**, **neon-glass cards** that **reveal the field on hover**, a **clean large VIONA wordmark**, and **vivid module color families** — global intelligence companion software, **not** gaming chaos.

### 14.2 Core principles

- **Dark cinematic base** — deep navy / ink canvas with controlled elevation; the field reads as cinematic space, not flat admin gray.
- **High-resolution global-network backgrounds** — licensed light-net / arc imagery stays sharp and legible; veils lighten masking so the network reads through, not a muddy wash.
- **Translucent glass cards** — deep glass fills over the field; utility tiers stay readable without opaque slabs.
- **Luminous borders** — crisp neon edges with inner top highlight and soft outer glow; one primary accent per card.
- **Vivid accent families** — violet, cyan, emerald, gold, and magenta/red each map to semantic roles (Section 14.3); avoid one-family cyan everywhere.
- **Hover reveal** — on web, cards lift slightly, borders and chips brighten, glow expands, and fill transparency increases so the **background network** shows through (**140–200ms** ease-out, no layout jump).
- **Clean large logo wordmark** — one confident VIONA lockup per viewport as brand anchor; no heavy boxed badge treatment.

### 14.3 Five accent families (role mapping)

| Family | Roles |
|--------|--------|
| **Violet / Purple** | AI, Academy, intelligence, learning |
| **Electric Blue / Cyan** | Travel, movement, discovery, network, language |
| **Emerald / Green** | Local, community, support, trust |
| **Gold / Yellow** | Premium, merchant, business, credits, primary CTA |
| **Magenta / Red** | SOS, urgent, safety, emergency **only** |

**Rule:** Magenta/red is **not** for normal service, commerce, or feature cards.

### 14.4 Card intensity tiers

| Tier | Role |
|------|------|
| **1 — Hero / Primary** | Largest impact, strongest scene and glow — hero bands, main route cards, destination cards |
| **2 — Service / Feature** | Vivid color identity, moderate scene glow, clear hover — Home world cards, Local / Travel / Academy / Business feature cards |
| **3 — Utility / Dense** | Restrained glow, readability first — forms, wallet, checkout, pricing, settings, legal, dense lists |

### 14.5 Card anatomy

Premium Global Light Network cards are **one continuous surface** (no nested slabs):

- Translucent **dark glass fill** over the network field  
- **Crisp neon border** with tier-appropriate edge weight  
- **Inner top highlight** (satin rim light)  
- **Soft outer glow** and **internal light wash** tied to the card’s accent family  
- **Glowing icon chip** and **luminous status chip**  
- **Arrow / CTA glow** on interactive affordances  
- **Hover lift** and **background reveal** — more field visible through the glass on web hover  

Optional subtle network line / node impression inside the card must not compete with title, body, or CTA.

### 14.6 Typography direction

- **Bright white / ivory titles** — confident hierarchy on navy fields  
- **Muted blue-gray body** — readable long copy without gray-on-navy failure  
- **Uppercase accent labels** with restrained tracking for section and status chips  
- **Role-based accent colors** on labels and chips (gold CTA, cyan travel, emerald local, violet academy, magenta SOS only)  
- **No unreadable glow text** — glow belongs on borders, chips, and icons; body copy stays crisp  

### 14.7 Logo direction

- **Clean VIONA wordmark** — large, wide-tracked lockup on dark global-network fields  
- **No heavy box** — transparent or softly blended surface; optional subtle gold glow, not a purple or navy plate  
- **One logo per viewport** — no duplicated lockups on the same screen  
- **Brand anchor, not small badge** — command rails and auth entry use confident scale; prefer reusable `VionaBrandLockup` variants when wired in rollout packs  

### 14.8 Anti-patterns

- Safe **flat navy boxes** with no field or network read  
- **Opaque cards** that hide the background instead of glass reveal  
- **Nested slabs** or card-inside-card stacks  
- **Duplicate logos** in one viewport  
- **All cards same cyan** — breaks module identity  
- **Red / magenta** outside SOS, safety, or documented urgent lanes  
- **Thick rainbow neon** or gaming-style frame overload  
- Unreadable **glowing body text** or generic dashboard chrome  

### 14.9 Rollout order

Apply in **small, screenshot-approved packs** — do not rewrite the whole app at once:

1. **Brand / logo** — wordmark lockup and rail placement  
2. **Background wiring** — per-universe global light-network assets on canvas  
3. **Home** — hero and world cards (Tier 1–2)  
4. **Local** — service constellation and commerce clarity (Tier 2)  
5. **Travel**  
6. **Academy**  
7. **Business / Merchant**  
8. **Account / Auth / Identity**  
9. **Wallet / Pricing / Checkout** — **Tier 3 utility** only; restrained glow and no commerce theater  

**Token foundation (this pack):** `src/components/viona/globalLightNetworkTokens.ts` — named accent, glass, border, glow, hover, tier, typography, logo, and role-mapping presets only (**no runtime behavior**, **no screen imports** until an explicit rollout pack). ## 10. Review checklist (per pack)

Before marking a visual pack complete:

1. Render path confirmed for the target route(s).  
2. Hero and licensed imagery unchanged if the pack says preserve.  
3. One primary accent per card; no banned motifs (orbs, rainbow, thick gaming-neon frames). Neon Glass cards follow tier and slab rules in `VIONA_NEON_GLASS_CARD_SYSTEM.md`.  
4. Status chips and hero/support copy pass Section 7.  
5. `npm run typecheck` and `npm run lint` pass.  
6. Desktop (and mobile where relevant) screenshots captured.  
7. `git status` clean of unintended files; **nothing staged** until user approves commit.
